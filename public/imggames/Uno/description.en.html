How to Play UNO

UNO is an exciting card game that children can learn in just a few minutes and enjoy for hours. The main goal is to be the first to get rid of all the cards in your hand. Follow these easy steps to start playing:

1. Get Ready

Deal seven cards to each player. Place the remaining deck face down in the center. Turn over the top card to begin the discard pile.

2. Match and Play

On your turn, look at the top card in the discard pile. You can play a card that matches the color (red, yellow, green, or blue) or the number (0-9). If you have a matching card, place it on top, and your turn ends.

3. Use Action Cards

Here's what each action card does:

  • Skip card: The next player misses their turn.
  • Reverse card: The playing order reverses direction.
  • Draw Two card: The next player draws two cards.
  • Wild card: You choose the color for the next turn.
  • Wild Draw Four: You choose a color, and the next player draws four cards (use this card only if you don't have a matching color).

4. What If You Can't Play?

If you can't play a card, draw one from the deck. If it fits, you can play it immediately. If not, keep it and wait for your next turn.

5. Shout “UNO!”

When you have only one card left, yell “UNO!” If you forget and someone catches you, draw two penalty cards.

6. Winning the Game

Keep taking turns, matching and discarding cards. The first player to discard all their cards wins!

Tips for Families and Friends

UNO Tips and Tricks for Kids

Do you dream of becoming the next UNO champion? Here are some simple tricks to help you improve your game:

Watch the Discard Pile

Pay attention to the cards everyone plays. When the color changes, you'll know the perfect card to play next!

Save Your Big Cards

Wild cards and Draw Four cards are your secret weapons. Keep them until the right moment—like when someone is about to win!

Use Skip and Reverse

If the player next to you is close to yelling “UNO!” surprise them with a Skip or Reverse card. It's a fun way to slow down their progress.

Keep Low Numbers Handy

Having a “1” or “2” in your hand can help you avoid taking extra cards when you can't play.

Count What's Played

This easy trick helps you guess which colors and numbers might still be in the deck. The more you pay attention, the better you'll be at predicting other players' moves.
